5x5 Go: C2 opening and intricacies

Werf et al. have shown the optimal play sequence for C2 openings in 5x5 Go, shown to the right. Black wins by 3 points.

Starting from scratch, bot has quickly learned that white should take C3 as the second move. As training progresses, it started exploring the optimal "cut" pattern (C2-C3-D3-D2); at one point, it even estimated that the final score of this pattern is between 2~3.

But in the next iteration, it seemed to have lost interest in this pattern. It explored alternatives at move #4, whose estimated score is -1. Clearly this outcome is more favorable to white, so it's reasonable that it would follow this path instead of playing D2 in move #4.

This shows the complexity of Go, even on a 5x5 board. To the right we show the likely optimal move #5 for black (E2 is the key). Now black is secure. White needs to worry about its own survival. Since it cannot play aggressive, the result is at least B+3.

This little exercise shows the challenge faced in Go training. It has no knowledge, at least not at this stage, of life and death. Many board valuations might be off, some completely wrong.

So we cannot just code up the conventional wisdom either. Self-play ended there with the result of B+1, although the actual margin is a bit larger if black captures the two isolated white stones.
